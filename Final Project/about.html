About Atari

inspire and entertain

Who We Are

Atari was founded in 1972 by Nolan Bushnell and Ted Dabney in Sunnyvale, California, marking the beginning of the video game industry's modern era. The idea for Atari began when Bushnell, having worked in the amusement industry, was inspired by the game Spacewar!, a complex but popular computer game created at MIT. Bushnell saw the potential to bring video games to the mass market in a more accessible, arcade-friendly format. However, he realized that Spacewar! was too intricate for the average person to play easily. This led him to develop a simpler, more intuitive game—Pong—which became Atari’s breakthrough product.

Atari remains a symbol of creativity, nostalgia, and progress, bridging the golden age of arcade gaming with the digital era.

Our Journey

In 1977, Atari made its first home console with the Atari 2600, which became one of the first widely successful home consoles. The 2600 introduced the concept of interchangeable game cartridges, which allowed players to switch between different games without buying a new system. This innovation helped the Atari 2600 become a cultural phenomenon, leading to millions of units sold and making home video gaming mainstream.

Over the decades, we’ve evolved During the early 1980s, Atari was at the height of its success, dominating arcades with games like Asteroids and Centipede. However, the 1983 video game crash and the release of poorly received games. led to a sharp decline in Atari’s fortunes. In 1984, Atari was sold to Jack Tramiel, founder of Commodore.

Despite attempts to recover with consoles like the Atari 7800 and the Jaguar, Atari struggled to compete with Nintendo and Sega. By the 2000s, Atari shifted to software and game publishing, focusing on digital and retro gaming. Today, Atari’s legacy endures through its classic games and nostalgic appeal, cementing its place as a pioneer in the gaming industry forever.

Our Mission

Atari’s mission has always been centered around pioneering innovation in the world of video games and entertainment technology. From its inception in 1972, the company aimed to create groundbreaking gaming experiences that would engage players and shape the future of interactive entertainment. Atari was determined to push the boundaries of technology, combining hardware and software innovation to provide fun and memorable experiences for players of all ages.

Atari’s mission evolved over time, focusing not just on arcade games and home consoles, but also on expanding into personal computing, mobile gaming, and digital experiences.

Today, Atari’s mission is to connect players to fun, engaging, and timeless games that pay homage to its iconic past, while embracing new platforms and creative gaming experiences for the future.
